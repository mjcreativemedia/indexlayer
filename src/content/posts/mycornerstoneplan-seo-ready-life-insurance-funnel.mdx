MyCornerstonePlan is the other side of the IndexLayer method.

Better Call Steve taught us what can go wrong when a publishing layer drifts. MyCornerstonePlan is the cleaner version: build the site so search readiness is present from day one.

## Why This Build Fits Astro

A life insurance broker does not need a heavy app stack to start ranking and converting. The business needs a trustworthy site, clear educational pages, fast loading, stable routes, and a funnel that makes the next step obvious.

<Callout>
For local service and advisory businesses, boring infrastructure is often the strongest infrastructure.
</Callout>

Astro fits because the most important output is static HTML that Google can crawl and people can read.

## The Funnel Shape

The site should make the path from education to contact feel calm and clear.

<ArchitectureDiagram
  title="SEO-ready funnel structure"
  steps={[
    "Educational entry page",
    "Trust content",
    "Offer explanation",
    "FAQ support",
    "Contact action",
    "Follow-up workflow"
  ]}
/>

That structure is simple, but it gives the site room to grow. Each future article can support the broker's authority and internally link toward the core funnel.

## The Publishing Standard

The build should treat every future page as a small publishing asset.

<CodeConfig label="Minimum page standard">

```txt
one clear search intent
one canonical URL
one readable HTML page
one next action
one internal link path back to the funnel
```

</CodeConfig>

This is how a small service business avoids the trap of building a pretty website with no publishing system underneath.

## What This Proves For IndexLayer

MyCornerstonePlan helps demonstrate that IndexLayer is not only a rescue practice. It is also a clean-build practice.

The goal is not to over-engineer the broker's site. The goal is to give the site a stable base: readable pages, clean structure, accurate metadata, and a content path that can compound over time.
